An actual fucking website.

You've seen the first one. Raw HTML. No CSS. Just text on a white void. Someone very angry on the internet said that was enough, and honestly? Fair point. Text is the whole point. The medium is the message, etc.

Then someone else came along with seven lines of CSS — a max-width, a font-size, some line-height — and said "look how much better it reads now." Also a great point. Readability matters. Give words room to breathe.

But here's what both of those websites got wrong.

They had one page.

One page is not a website. One page is a sticky note. It's a pamphlet you hand someone at the airport and then sprint away. It says one thing and it's done with you forever.

A website has pages. It has an about page so people know you're a human and not an AI. It has a blog so your thoughts exist somewhere other than a thread on a platform that will be bankrupt, irrelevant, or acquired by a bored billionaire by Thursday. It has a /now page because Derek Sivers was right. It has a contact page with your email address, because how the hell else is anyone supposed to reach you — a contact form that sends data to three different analytics companies?

You can have all of that. With a nav that is just a list of links. With a layout that is just centering. With CSS you could read in under a minute.

This is that website.

Look at that nav up there. Five links. Each one goes to a page. That's it. That's the whole architecture. No framework, no hydration boundary, no lighthouse score celebration tweet. Just pages.

This entire site is under 15KB uncompressed. Your cookie consent banner is larger than this website.

Go read the about page. Then come back and tell me we need a 200KB JavaScript bundle to display biographical text.
